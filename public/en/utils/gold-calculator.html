How is the value of gold calculated?

The international gold price is expressed in US dollars per troy ounce (31.1 grams). To get the price per gram in euros, divide by 31.1 and convert the currency. Once you have the price per gram of pure gold (24K), the value of your piece depends on its purity:

Value = Weight (g) × 24K Price (€/g) × Purity factor

Why does the jeweller offer less than the market value?

Gold buyers apply a discount on the market price to cover their operating costs and make a profit. They typically pay between 70% and 90% of the market value. It is also worth noting that the actual weight of a piece of jewellery includes other alloy metals, settings and stones that have no value as gold.

Frequently asked questions about gold value

How is the value of gold calculated?

Multiply the weight in grams by the current pure gold price (24K) and by the purity factor of the piece. For example, 10 grams of 18K gold with gold at €142/g would be worth: 10 × 142 × 0.75 = €1,065.

What do 18K, 14K and 24K mean in gold?

Karats indicate purity. 24K is pure gold. 18K means 18 out of every 24 parts are gold (75% purity). 14K equals 58.5% and 9K equals 37.5%.

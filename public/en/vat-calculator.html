How to Calculate VAT in 2026?

Calculating VAT is an essential process for both businesses and consumers. Value Added Tax (VAT) is a consumption tax placed on a product whenever value is added at each stage of the supply chain.

Current VAT Rates in the UK

  • Standard Rate (20%): Applicable to most goods and services (electronics, clothing, professional services).
  • Reduced Rate (5%): Applied to specific goods like children's car seats and home energy.
  • Zero Rate (0%): Applicable to most food items, books, and children's clothes.

Formulas to Add and Exclude VAT

To work with VAT manually, you can use the following mathematical formulas:

Add VAT: Total Price = Net Amount × (1 + VAT Rate/100)
Exclude VAT: Net Amount = Total Price / (1 + VAT Rate/100)

Frequently Asked Questions about VAT

What is the difference between adding and excluding VAT?

Adding VAT is done on the net price (base amount) to find the total. Excluding (or stripping) VAT is performed on the final price (Gross) to find out how much corresponds to the tax and how much to the real value of the product.

What VAT rates exist in the UK?

There are currently three rates:

  • Standard (20%): Applies to most products and services.
  • Reduced (5%): Energy, heating, and some safety products.
  • Zero (0%): Essential items like food, books, and children's clothing.
How to calculate VAT manually?

To add 20% VAT, multiply the net amount by 1.20. To extract it from a final price, divide the total by 1.20. The difference between the total and that result will be the VAT amount.
